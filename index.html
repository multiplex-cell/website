What We Do

Research

We combine cutting-edge genome editing tools with scalable cell engineering approaches to address unmet clinical needs in hematology and oncology.

✂

Large-Scale Genome Engineering

We develop and apply high-throughput platforms to introduce tens to hundreds of precise genomic modifications in primary human cells, enabling combinatorial dissection of gene function and synthetic cellular programs.

🧬

CRISPR & Prime Editing

From nuclease-based knockout screens to the precision of prime editing and base editing, we push the boundaries of what can be written into the genome without double-strand breaks—minimizing off-target risk for therapeutic applications.

💎

HSC Gene Therapies

Hematopoietic stem and progenitor cells (HSPCs) are the root of lifelong blood production. We engineer HSCs with curative genetic corrections for hemoglobinopathies and primary immunodeficiencies, optimizing editing efficiency while preserving engraftment potential.

🛡

CAR-T Cell Development

We design next-generation chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) constructs and use multiplex editing to enhance T-cell fitness, tumor infiltration, and resistance to immunosuppression—advancing solid tumor and hematological malignancy targets.

RF
Principal Investigator

Raphael Ferreira, PhD

Raphael leads the Multiplex Cell Engineering Lab at DTU Health Tech. His research integrates large-scale CRISPR screening, prime editing, and cell therapy design to create curative genomic medicines. He holds a PhD in molecular biology and completed postdoctoral training focused on hematopoietic stem cell gene editing. Raphael is passionate about building open, collaborative science and bridging academic discovery with clinical translation.
